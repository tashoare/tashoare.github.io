Calendula oil was my first foray into using flowers/herbs for skincare. We grew them in the polytunnel in big numbers on the Lancaster community farm... they are a great trap to attract aphids away from the valuable crops. They also bring beneficial insects inside with their vibrant orange display, a quality which makes them one of my favourite cut flowers.

After a bit of research I found out the flower is also hypo-allergenic with a bunch of skin-healing properties (I really love this plant), and as someone who suffers with sensitive skin and acne I decided to try making some oil.

I picked a bag of flowerheads and then left them to dry on a sunny windowsill for about 2 weeks, until they were papery. I then removed the petals and put them in a disinfected jar, completely covering them with almond oil (maybe mixed with some olive oil to keep the cost down for a budget student). I left it on the windowsill for another ~6 weeks so the petals could infuse, shaking the jar whenever I remembered.
